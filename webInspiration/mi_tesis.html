Quantum Dynamics and the Architecture of Artificial Neural Networks

The integration of quantum computing principles into neural network architectures represents a paradigm shift in computational efficiency. As we explore the stochastic nature of gradient descent in high-dimensional spaces, we find that quantum tunneling effects offer a unique perspective on escaping local minima—a challenge that has historically plagued deep learning models.

Our research indicates that by leveraging qubits for weight representation, we can theoretically achieve an exponential increase in data density. However, the decoherence of these states remains the primary obstacle in maintaining consistent predictive accuracy. In the following sections, we will delineate the mathematical framework required to stabilize these interference patterns during the backpropagation phase.
